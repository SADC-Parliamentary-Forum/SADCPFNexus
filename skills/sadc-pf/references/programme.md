## Programme Implementation Form Structure

The Programme Implementation Form is completed for each planned SADC PF programme activity
(workshop, seminar, conference, capacity building event, etc.) and serves as the planning
and budget authorisation document.

**Key sections** (verify against current form template):

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Activity Details** | Activity name, type, dates, venue, host country/institution |
| **Objectives** | Programme objectives aligned to SADC PF Strategic Plan |
| **Participants** | Number and category (MPs, staff, experts, CSO, media, etc.) |
| **Budget Lines** | Itemised cost breakdown by category with unit costs and totals |
| **Funding Source** | Budget line code; donor project code (if donor-funded) |
| **DSA & Allowances** | DSA for each participant category; rate type applied |
| **Logistics** | Venue, accommodation, interpretation, transport arrangements |
| **Consultants/Resource Persons** | Names, roles, fees, contracts required |
| **Conflict of Interest** | COI declarations from relevant staff and consultants |
| **Approvals** | Programme Officer → Head of Programme → Finance → Director → SG |

## Budget Line Allocation & Justification

Each programme budget must be structured with clear, justified line items:

| Budget Category | Typical Items | Justification Required |
|----------------|--------------|----------------------|
| DSA / Per Diem | Participant daily allowances by rate type | Number of participants × days × applicable rate |
| Accommodation | Hotel costs (where not included in DSA) | Quoted rate × nights × participants |
| Air travel | Return airfares by origin | Quoted/estimated fare × number of travellers |
| Ground transport | Airport transfers, local transport | Distance/quote-based |
| Interpretation | Per-language per-day rate | Number of languages × days × rate |
| Documentation | Printing, translation, production | Per-unit costs |
| Consultants/Resource Persons | Fees + DSA + travel | Per consultant, per contract |
| Venue | Conference room rental | Quoted rate × days |
| Contingency | Allowable percentage of total budget | Per donor/SADC PF rules (typically 5–10%) |
| Miscellaneous | Small unspecified costs | Must not exceed policy ceiling |

**Budget justification rule**: Every line item must have a unit cost and quantity that
supports the total. Lump sums without justification will be queried by Finance.

**Split purchasing**: If a programme requires procurement above the approved supplier
threshold, the correct procurement route must be followed — do not split across budget lines
to avoid a competitive tender.

## Funding Source Structuring

- All activities must be mapped to an approved budget line in the SADC PF annual budget
  or to a specific donor project
- If donor-funded: identify the donor project code, applicable procurement/DSA rules
  (donor rules may differ from SADC PF internal rules — the more restrictive applies)
- Co-funded activities: clearly separate SADC PF contribution vs. donor/partner contribution
- If a host organisation is co-funding: document what the host covers (accommodation,
  meals, transport) — this affects DSA rate type for participants

## DSA Rate Calculation & Variance Justification

DSA for programme participants follows the same rate structure as staff travel:

| Rate | What it covers |
|------|---------------|
| Rate 1 | Full DSA (accommodation + meals + incidentals) |
| Rate 2 | Meals + incidentals (accommodation paid by SADC PF/host separately) |
| Rate 3 | Incidentals only (accommodation and meals fully provided) |

**Variance justification**: If the DSA claimed differs from the planned amount (e.g., fewer
participants attended, activity was shorter), document:
- Original plan vs. actual
- Reason for variance
- Adjusted financial impact
- Finance approval for the variance

**Participant category differentiation**: Different participant categories may attract
different DSA rates (e.g., MPs vs. staff vs. external experts). Document the category and
applicable rate for each group.

## Participant Logistics Planning

**Participant tracking checklist**:
- [ ] Confirmed participant list (name, country, category, dietary/accessibility needs)
- [ ] Visa requirements identified and support letters issued
- [ ] Flight booking arrangements confirmed (SADC PF booking or self-booking with
  reimbursement)
- [ ] Airport transfer arrangements confirmed
- [ ] Hotel bookings confirmed (room allocation, check-in/check-out dates)
- [ ] DSA/allowance calculation completed per participant
- [ ] Payment method agreed (cash advance, bank transfer, on-site payment)
- [ ] Attendance sheet prepared for signature at the event

## Consultant & Resource Person Contracting

All consultants and resource persons engaged for SADC PF activities must have a formal
contract in place **before** the activity.

**Contracting requirements**:
- Terms of Reference (ToR) — scope of work, deliverables, timeline
- Agreed fee/honorarium (within approved budget line)
- Travel and DSA arrangements (if applicable)
- Conflict of interest declaration signed
- Tax/withholding tax compliance (per Namibian tax requirements)
- Invoice/receipt procedure agreed

**Procurement route**: Consultant engagement is subject to SADC PF procurement thresholds
(see `procurement.md`). If the total engagement value exceeds the relevant threshold, the
appropriate procurement method must be followed before engagement.

**Resource persons** (speakers, facilitators, panellists providing input, not delivering
a consultancy): May be engaged on an honorarium basis with a simpler letter of engagement,
but must still declare any conflict of interest.

## Interpretation & Multilingual Coordination

SADC PF operates in three official languages: **English, French, Portuguese**.

**Interpretation planning**:
- Identify which languages are required based on participant nationalities
- Book interpreters (SADC PF roster or contracted external interpreters) for each
  language pair required
- Arrange interpretation equipment (booths, receivers, headsets) where consecutive
  interpretation is insufficient
- Budget for: interpreter fees + DSA (if travelling) + equipment hire

**Document translation**:
- Key documents (agenda, concept note, background papers, resolutions) must be
  translated into all working languages used at the activity
- Translation timeline: allow sufficient lead time (typically 5–10 working days
  for professional translation)
- Confirm translator qualifications and rate against approved rates
- Translation costs must appear as a specific budget line

## Venue & Accommodation Planning

**Venue requirements checklist**:
- [ ] Seating capacity confirmed (plenary + breakout rooms if needed)
- [ ] Interpretation facilities available (booths or equipment hire arranged)
- [ ] AV equipment available (projector, screens, microphones, recording if required)
- [ ] Accessibility requirements met (PWD access)
- [ ] Catering arrangements confirmed (refreshments, meals — note impact on DSA rate)
- [ ] Internet/connectivity adequate (especially for hybrid sessions)
- [ ] Venue booking contract signed with clear cancellation terms

**Hotel/accommodation**:
- Preferred accommodation list or SADC PF approved hotels (consult Admin/Finance)
- Hotel invoice addressed to SADC PF for direct payment (where possible)
- Room allocation list prepared before check-in
- If accommodation is paid by SADC PF: participants receive Rate 2 or Rate 3 DSA
  (not Rate 1 — deduct accommodation component)

## Hybrid Meeting & Digital Platform Coordination

For hybrid (in-person + virtual) events:

**Technical requirements**:
- Stable internet connection at the venue confirmed
- Video conferencing platform selected (Zoom, Teams, Webex — per SADC PF IT guidance)
- Technical support assigned (on-site and virtual moderator/co-host)
- Test session conducted before the event with all participants
- Recording arrangements confirmed (if meeting is to be recorded per Rules of Procedure)

**Logistics for virtual participants**:
- Platform link/login details distributed in advance
- Time zone confirmed for all virtual participants
- Interpretation available in digital format if required
- Separate virtual attendance register maintained

## Conflict of Interest Declaration

All staff, consultants, and evaluators involved in programme planning or procurement must
complete a **Conflict of Interest Declaration** before participation.

**COI declaration must cover**:
- Any personal, financial, or professional relationship with suppliers/bidders
- Any family/personal relationship with participants or beneficiaries that could influence decisions
- Any secondary employment or consultancy that conflicts with the activity

**If a conflict is declared**:
1. Document the declared conflict
2. The conflicted person must recuse themselves from the relevant decision
3. A replacement reviewer/approver must be appointed
4. The recusal is recorded in the programme file

## Documentation & Translation Oversight

**Required programme documents** (to be filed after each activity):
- Signed attendance register (original, not photocopied)
- Activity report (prepared by Programme Officer within 10 working days of event)
- Financial retirement/liquidation (DSA payments reconciled against attendance)
- Evaluation forms (if used)
- Photographs / evidence of activity (where appropriate)
- Minutes or proceedings (if applicable)
- All translated documents (in all working languages used)

## Stakeholder Coordination (Regional Level)

SADC PF activities involve stakeholders across 16 member states + regional institutions.

**Coordination principles**:
- Official invitations must go through the SADC PF Secretariat (not directly from
  Programme Officers to national parliaments — route via SG office)
- Confirmation from national parliaments of participant nomination must be received
  and filed before travel/DSA is committed
- Regional partner institutions (AU Pan-African Parliament, SADC Secretariat, IPU, etc.)
  require formal communication from SG level
- Any commitments made to regional stakeholders (funding, hosting, co-organisation)
  must be authorised by SG before communication
