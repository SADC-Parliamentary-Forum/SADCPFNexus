## Salary Advance — Eligibility & Conditions

A salary advance is a short-term, interest-free advance against a staff member's future salary.

**Eligibility requirements** (verify current Admin Rules for specifics):
- Staff member must be on a confirmed appointment (not on probation, unless Admin Rules
  permit)
- No outstanding unretired imprests (travel advances) — advance blocked if any open imprest
  exists
- No default on any previous salary advance repayment
- Advance purpose must fall within permitted categories (consult Admin Rules)

**Permitted purposes** (typical — verify against current Admin Rules):
- Medical emergency (self or immediate family)
- School fees (children)
- Housing-related emergency
- Other documented personal emergency approved by HR Director/SG

## 50% Salary Threshold Compliance

**Core rule**: The total of all outstanding salary advances and deductions at any time must
not cause the staff member's net take-home pay to fall below **50% of their gross salary**.

**Compliance check formula**:
```
Net Pay After Deductions = Gross Salary − Total Committed Deductions
Minimum Net Pay Required = Gross Salary × 50%

If Net Pay After Deductions < Minimum Net Pay Required:
  → BLOCKED — advance cannot be approved until existing deductions reduce
```

**Steps for Finance to perform compliance check**:
1. Obtain staff member's current gross monthly salary
2. List all current monthly deductions (medical aid, existing advances, etc.)
3. Add proposed new advance monthly repayment instalment
4. Confirm net pay ≥ 50% of gross salary
5. Document result on the application form with Finance certification

## Application Process

**Salary Advance Application Form** must capture:
- Staff member name, employee number, department
- Amount requested (in figures and words)
- Purpose of advance (with brief justification)
- Proposed repayment period (number of months)
- Monthly deduction instalment amount
- 50% threshold compliance check result (Finance to certify)
- Current payslip or salary confirmation attached

**Approval routing**:
1. Staff member completes and signs application
2. Line Manager recommends (or declines with reason)
3. HR certifies eligibility and checks no conflicting advance/outstanding imprest
4. Finance certifies 50% threshold compliance
5. Director recommends
6. SG approves (or Finance Director, per delegation — verify current Admin Rules)

**Segregation of duties**: The staff member cannot approve their own advance. The certifying
Finance officer cannot be the same person as the authorising Director/SG.

## Repayment Scheduling

- Repayment is by **monthly payroll deduction** over the agreed period
- Repayment schedule must be signed by the staff member and attached to the approved application
- The schedule must show: month, instalment amount, balance outstanding, cumulative repaid
- Early repayment is permitted and encouraged
- On separation from service (resignation, contract end, etc.): any outstanding advance
  balance is recovered from **terminal benefits** (gratuity, leave encashment, etc.)

**Repayment schedule format**:
```
Staff Member: [Name]    Employee No: [X]    Advance Amount: NAD [X]
Monthly Instalment: NAD [X]    Repayment Period: [X] months

Month | Instalment | Cumulative Paid | Balance Outstanding
  1   |  NAD [X]  |    NAD [X]      |    NAD [X]
 ...  |           |                 |
[Last]|  NAD [X]  |    NAD [X]      |    NAD 0
```

## Payroll Deduction Governance

Deductions from staff salaries are ONLY permitted for the following authorised categories:

| Deduction Type | Required Documentation |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Medical aid contributions | Enrolment/election form signed by staff member |
| Salary advance repayment | Approved advance application + signed repayment schedule |
| Imprest/travel advance recovery | Approved retirement statement showing overpayment amount |
| Overpayment recovery | Written authorisation from Finance Director; recovery schedule agreed with staff |
| Other authorised deductions | **Written consent signed by the staff member** |

**BLOCKED**: Any deduction without documented authorisation must be flagged and not processed.

---

## Permitted Deduction Categories

Summary of all permitted payroll deductions:
1. Medical aid (SADC PF employer-employee contribution arrangement per Admin Rules)
2. Salary advances (approved, within 50% threshold)
3. Travel imprest recovery (overpayments per retirement statement)
4. Tax obligations (where applicable per Namibian tax law)
5. Staff association/union contributions (where applicable and with written consent)
6. Any court-ordered deduction (with official court order documentation)

**Non-permitted without explicit consent**: Any deduction not in the above categories
requires the staff member's written, dated consent before processing.

## Payroll Controls & Segregation of Duties

**Core rule**: The person who **prepares** payroll changes ≠ the person who **approves** them.

| Control | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Segregation of duties | Preparer and approver must be different individuals |
| Audit log | Every change requires: employee ID, change type, old value, new value, authorisation ref, preparer name/date, approver name/date |
| Variance analysis | Month-on-month payroll comparison — explain any material variance |
| Exception list | Missing authorisations, unusual variances, SoD violations → report to Finance Director |
| Lock after approval | Approved payroll run is locked — changes require new version with authorisation |

**Month-on-month variance**: Flag any unexplained variance exceeding a material threshold
as an EXCEPTION. All variances must have a documented explanation before the payroll is
certified and paid.

## Gratuity & Benefits Administration

Gratuity is a terminal benefit payable to staff on completion of a contract or on separation.

**General principles** (verify exact rates and conditions in Admin Rules):
- Gratuity is typically calculated as a percentage of total pensionable emoluments earned
  over the contract period
- Eligibility conditions (minimum service period, type of contract) are set in Admin Rules
- Outstanding deductions (salary advances, unretired imprests) are recovered from gratuity
  before payment
- Gratuity is processed after all deductions are netted off; Finance Director authorises
  final payment
- Gratuity calculation must be documented and verified by Finance before SG approval

**Other terminal benefits** (where applicable per Admin Rules):
- Leave encashment (unused accrued annual leave)
- Repatriation allowance
- Any other contractual terminal entitlement

## Remuneration Governance

- All salary scales, allowances, and remuneration structures are set by the SADC PF
  governing organs (Executive Committee / Plenary) — Finance/HR cannot unilaterally change
  remuneration
- Any salary adjustment requires a formal decision of the appropriate organ, documented in
  the resolution register
- Increments, promotions, or reclassifications must be processed with: the governing organ
  decision reference, HR confirmation, Finance certification, and SG approval
- Remuneration information is confidential — do not disclose one staff member's salary to
  another

## Financial Risk Assessment (Staff Advances)

When assessing a salary advance application, Finance must consider:

| Risk Factor | Assessment |
|-------------|-----------|
| Outstanding imprests | Block advance if any unretired travel advance exists |
| Existing advance balance | Include in 50% threshold calculation |
| Length of remaining contract | Short remaining contract = higher recovery risk; recommend shorter repayment period or full upfront recovery from terminal benefits |
| Separation risk | If staff member is on notice / contract ending soon, require Finance Director approval and adjust repayment accordingly |
| Multiple advances | Flag if staff member has a pattern of advance requests — recommend HR review |
