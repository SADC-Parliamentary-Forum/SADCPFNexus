## Procurement Methods Overview

SADC PF uses four procurement methods depending on value and complexity:

| Method | When Applied | Key Features |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Open (Competitive) Tender** | Above N$100,000; or donor-mandated | Public advertisement; all qualified suppliers can bid; maximum transparency |
| **Restricted Tender** | Where open tender is impractical; specific technical requirements; limited supplier market | Shortlist of pre-qualified suppliers invited; must justify restriction |
| **Simplified Procurement (3 Quotations)** | N$10,001 – N$100,000 | Minimum 3 written quotations; comparative evaluation |
| **Negotiated Procurement** | Emergency; sole source; contract extension | Must justify why competitive process not possible; documented approval required |

**Default rule**: Always use the most competitive method warranted by the value. Use
of a less competitive method where a more competitive one is required must be formally
justified and approved by the Finance Director (and SG for significant deviations).

## SADC PF Procurement Thresholds

| Estimated Value (NAD) | Required Method |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| Up to N$10,000 | Approved Supplier List — select from approved suppliers only |
| N$10,001 – N$100,000 | Minimum **3 written quotations** |
| Above N$100,000 | **Open competitive tender** |
| Any value — Donor-funded | Apply donor-specific procedures (Sida, EU, etc.) in addition to above |

> **Important**: These thresholds apply to the **total value of a procurement**, not to
> individual invoices or line items. See Split Purchase Detection below.

## SIDA Procurement Principles

For all Sida-funded procurement, the following principles apply and are non-negotiable:

| Principle | What it requires |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Open & fair competition** | All qualified and interested suppliers must have an equal opportunity to compete; no preferential access |
| **Transparency** | Procurement process, evaluation criteria, and award decision must be documented and available for review/audit |
| **Equal treatment** | All bidders assessed against identical criteria; no information shared with some bidders that is not shared with all |
| **Non-discrimination** | No exclusion based on nationality, size, or other non-merit factors |
| **Proportionality** | Procurement requirements (documentation, procedures) must be proportionate to the value and complexity of the procurement |
| **Conflict of interest avoidance** | All evaluators must declare and recuse from any conflict of interest before the process begins |

These principles apply **in addition to** SADC PF internal thresholds, not instead of them.
Where Sida rules are more stringent than SADC PF internal rules, Sida rules prevail.

## Terms of Reference (ToR) Development

A ToR is required for all procurements involving consultants, service providers, or complex
goods/works. A well-written ToR protects SADC PF by making expectations clear and measurable.

**ToR must include**:

1. **Background**: Brief context of the SADC PF programme/activity requiring the service
2. **Objective**: What the procurement aims to achieve (specific, measurable)
3. **Scope of Work**: Detailed description of tasks/deliverables expected
4. **Deliverables**: List of specific outputs (reports, training sessions, tools, etc.)
   with due dates
5. **Timeline**: Duration of engagement, key milestones
6. **Required Qualifications**: Academic qualifications, professional experience, relevant
   technical skills (minimum and preferred)
7. **Reporting Arrangements**: Who the consultant/provider reports to; review and approval
   of deliverables process
8. **Budget/Indicative Fee** (optional but recommended for consultant ToRs)
9. **Evaluation Criteria**: How proposals will be scored (technical + financial weighting)

**Review of ToR**: ToR must be reviewed by the Programme Officer, Head of Programme,
and Finance before publication/distribution to bidders.

## Evaluation Process

**Evaluation Committee**:
- Minimum 3 members (odd number recommended to avoid tie votes)
- Must include: technical evaluator(s) + Finance representative
- All members must sign COI declaration before evaluation begins
- No evaluator may communicate with bidders during the evaluation period

**Two-envelope system** (for complex procurements):
1. **Technical proposal** evaluated first (without financial information visible)
2. **Financial proposal** opened only for technically qualified bidders

**Evaluation scoring** (example — adjust weights per ToR):

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Technical approach / methodology | 30% |
| Relevant experience (organisation) | 25% |
| Key personnel qualifications | 25% |
| Financial proposal | 20% |
| **Total** | **100%** |

**Minimum technical threshold**: Bidders scoring below 70% on technical criteria are
typically disqualified before financial evaluation (confirm threshold in each ToR).

**Evaluation report**: Must document all scores, justifications, and the recommended
awardee with rationale. Signed by all committee members.

## Eligibility Screening

Before evaluating proposals on merit, screen for eligibility:

| Eligibility Check | Requirement |
|------------------|------------|
| Legal status | Registered legal entity (company registration, certificate of incorporation, or equivalent) |
| Tax compliance | Current tax clearance certificate or equivalent |
| Not debarred | Supplier is not on any debarment/exclusion list (SADC PF or Sida blacklist) |
| Not conflicted | Supplier has no disqualifying conflict of interest with SADC PF or the programme |
| Response completeness | All required documents submitted; bid submitted on time |

**Automatic disqualification**: Bids that are late, incomplete (missing mandatory documents),
or submitted by a debarred/conflicted supplier are disqualified without further evaluation.

## Economic & Financial Capacity Assessment

For significant procurements, verify that the selected supplier can financially sustain the
contract:

| Assessment Area | Documentation Required |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Financial statements | Audited accounts for the last 2–3 years |
| Turnover | Annual turnover commensurate with contract value |
| Banking references | Bank reference letter confirming financial standing |
| Insurance | Relevant professional indemnity, public liability insurance |
| No insolvency | Confirmation that supplier is not under administration/liquidation |

**Proportionality**: Financial capacity requirements must be proportionate to the contract.
Do not require large financial statements for small contracts — this discriminates against
smaller suppliers unnecessarily.

---

## Technical & Professional Capacity Evaluation

| Assessment Area | What to verify |
|----------------|---------------|
| Previous similar assignments | References with contact details; at least 2–3 comparable projects |
| Key personnel CVs | Qualifications, years of experience, specific relevant expertise |
| Methodology | Logical, feasible approach to the assignment; demonstrates understanding of the ToR |
| Management capacity | Ability to manage the contract size and complexity |
| Language capacity | Proficiency in SADC PF working languages as required (English/French/Portuguese) |

**Reference checks**: For shortlisted/preferred bidders, contact provided references to
verify performance on past assignments. Document reference check outcomes.

## Conflict of Interest Mitigation

**COI declaration is mandatory** for all evaluation committee members before evaluation begins.

**Types of conflicts to declare**:
- Personal/family relationship with a bidder's principals or staff
- Financial interest in a bidder
- Previous employment with a bidder (within a defined exclusion period — check policy)
- Any circumstance that could influence impartial assessment

**Recusal protocol**:
1. Conflicted evaluator declares the conflict in writing
2. Conflicted evaluator is removed from evaluation of that bidder/component
3. A replacement evaluator is appointed
4. Recusal is documented in the evaluation report

**Post-award COI**: If a conflict is discovered after award, escalate immediately to
Finance Director and SG. Do not proceed with contract signing until resolved.

## Contract Award Governance

**Award process**:
1. Evaluation Committee recommends award (with signed evaluation report)
2. Finance Director reviews and approves
3. SG approves (or delegates to Finance Director within authorised threshold — verify)
4. Unsuccessful bidders notified (per Sida/SADC PF transparency requirements)
5. Contract signed by both parties before any work begins
6. Contract filed in procurement file (original); copy to Finance for payment processing

**Contract must include**:
- Scope of work and deliverables (from ToR)
- Timeline and milestones
- Fee structure and payment schedule
- Acceptance/approval criteria for deliverables
- Termination provisions
- Dispute resolution
- Anti-corruption clause (mandatory for Sida-funded contracts)

**No work before contract**: No supplier/consultant may commence work or receive payment
before a signed contract exists.

## Split Purchase Detection

**Definition**: Deliberately dividing a single procurement into smaller parts to stay below
a threshold requiring a more competitive process.

**Red flags to check**:
- Multiple requisitions for the same type of goods/services within a short period
- Same supplier used for multiple small purchases that together form a logical whole
- Requisition values suspiciously close to threshold limits
- Combined total of related purchases exceeds the applicable threshold

**Response when suspected**:
> _"Potential split purchasing detected — the following items appear to constitute a single
> procurement of approximately N$[estimated total]: [list items]. This would require
> [correct procurement method] under SADC PF procurement policy. Escalate to Finance Director
> before proceeding."_

## Documentation Control & Transparency Compliance

**Procurement file must contain** (in chronological order):
- [ ] Approved purchase requisition with specifications/ToR
- [ ] Procurement method justification (if not standard method for the value)
- [ ] Advertisement or invitation to tender (with distribution evidence)
- [ ] All bids/quotations received (original, with submission date)
- [ ] COI declarations from all evaluation committee members
- [ ] Evaluation report (signed by all committee members)
- [ ] Award approval (Finance Director + SG)
- [ ] Notification to unsuccessful bidders
- [ ] Signed contract
- [ ] All correspondence with bidders/suppliers
- [ ] Payment vouchers and invoices (once contract is in execution)
- [ ] Evidence of delivery/acceptance of goods or services

**Retention**: Procurement files must be retained for a minimum period per SADC PF/donor
records management policy (typically 7–10 years). Do not destroy procurement records
without authorisation.
