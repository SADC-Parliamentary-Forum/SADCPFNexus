## SADC PF Institutional Structure

The SADC Parliamentary Forum is an inter-parliamentary body of the Southern African
Development Community (SADC), bringing together parliaments of SADC member states.

**Principal organs** (per SADC PF Constitution):

| Organ | Role |
|-------|------|
| **Plenary Assembly** | Highest decision-making body; full membership assembly |
| **Executive Committee (EXCO)** | Governance body between Plenaries; implements Plenary decisions |
| **Standing Committees** | Thematic committees that examine issues and make recommendations to Plenary |
| **Secretariat** | Administrative and technical arm; headed by the Secretary General |

**Member states**: SADC PF brings together parliamentary delegations from SADC member states.
Each member state national parliament is represented by a delegation in the Plenary.

---

## Legal Personality & Status

- The SADC PF has an international legal personality as confirmed in its Constitution
- It can enter into contracts, own property, and be a party to legal proceedings in its
  own right
- The SADC PF Secretariat is headquartered in Windhoek, Namibia
- Staff of the Secretariat operate under Namibian law (employment, tax) while serving
  the international organisation
- External agreements (MoUs, host country agreements) require SG signature and, for
  significant commitments, EXCO/Plenary authorisation

## Plenary Assembly

The Plenary Assembly is the supreme decision-making organ.

**Composition**: Delegations from all member state parliaments
**Sessions**: Meets at least once per year (per Constitution — verify current Rules)

**Functions**:
- Adopt the SADC PF budget
- Elect the President and Vice Presidents
- Adopt resolutions on governance and parliamentary matters
- Amend the Constitution (specific majority required — see Rules)
- Receive and consider reports from EXCO, Standing Committees, and Secretariat

**Pre-Plenary preparation** (Secretariat responsibilities):
- Provisional agenda circulated with required notice period
- Background papers and committee reports prepared and translated
- Credentials of delegates verified
- Quorum confirmed at session opening

> **Organ-specific quorum and voting thresholds**: Load current Rules of Procedure for
> the exact provisions — do not assume thresholds without a policy basis.

---

## Executive Committee (EXCO)

**Composition**: Officers of the SADC PF (President, Vice Presidents) + other members
as defined in the Rules of Procedure/Constitution

**Functions**:
- Meets between Plenary sessions to conduct ongoing governance
- Implements Plenary decisions and resolutions
- May make decisions within the scope delegated by Plenary
- Oversees the Secretariat and the SG
- Reviews and amends the Constitution (as authorised)
- Approves policies, budgets, and staffing matters within its mandate

**EXCO decisions**: Documented in minutes and resolution register. Decisions that fall
within Plenary mandate cannot be made unilaterally by EXCO — must be referred back.

---

## Standing Committees

**Role**: Examine specific thematic areas and submit reports/recommendations to Plenary.

**Common Standing Committees** (verify current committee structure with Secretariat):
- Gender Equality, Women and Young People
- Democracy, Governance, Human Rights and Humanitarian Issues
- Trade, Industry, Finance and Investment
- Food, Agriculture and Natural Resources
- Legal Affairs and Human Rights
- HIV/AIDS and Health

**Committee functions**:
- Conduct thematic inquiries and research
- Invite expert witnesses and briefings
- Draft model laws, resolutions, and recommendations for Plenary
- Oversee implementation of Plenary resolutions in their thematic area

**Committee meetings**: Require proper notice, quorum, and minutes per the Rules of Procedure.

---

## Secretariat

**Headed by**: Secretary General (SG), appointed by the Plenary

**Functions**:
- Day-to-day administration and management
- Support to all SADC PF organs
- Programme implementation
- Financial management (supervised by SG)
- Staff management and HR
- Liaison with member parliaments and external partners

**SG authority**: SG is the chief administrative officer and authorises expenditures,
contracts, and official communications within the limits set by governing organs.
Significant commitments (above delegated thresholds) require EXCO or Plenary authorisation.

## Quorum Rules

> **Critical note**: Specific quorum numbers (e.g., "simple majority of members", "two-thirds")
> for each organ are set in the Rules of Procedure. Always load the relevant Rules chapter
> before advising on quorum. Do not assume a number without a source.

**General principles**:
- A meeting cannot validly proceed to business or take binding decisions without quorum
- Quorum is assessed at the start of the session and must be maintained
- If quorum is lost during a meeting: record the exact time and moment of quorum loss;
  business after that point is deferred or treated as informal
- Any member may raise a point of order on quorum

**Quorum monitoring checklist**:
- [ ] Attendance list completed at session opening
- [ ] Quorum confirmed before business begins (noted in the record)
- [ ] Departures/arrivals tracked; quorum re-confirmed after any significant movement
- [ ] Quorum break (if any) noted in minutes with exact time

## Agenda & Notice Requirements

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Notice period | Per Rules of Procedure for the specific organ (verify chapter) |
| Agenda items | Submitted by members/Secretariat by a deadline before the notice period |
| Agenda distribution | Circulated with all supporting documents within the notice period |
| Emergency/late items | Requires agreement of members present to add; specific procedure per Rules |
| Order Paper | Final agenda distributed at the opening; no changes without members' agreement |

**Provisional agenda vs. adopted agenda**: The provisional agenda is circulated in advance;
members may propose additions/deletions; the final agenda is adopted by the organ at the
start of the session.

---

## Voting Rules

> Specific thresholds (simple majority, two-thirds, consensus) vary by organ and decision type.
> Always load the relevant Rules of Procedure chapter before advising on voting requirements.

**General principles**:
- Consensus is the preferred method where achievable
- Where a vote is taken: the method must be agreed/specified (show of hands, roll call,
  secret ballot)
- Roll call vote: recorded by name in the minutes (for/against/abstain per member)
- Secret ballot: where constitutionally required (e.g., elections) — paper ballots, tellers appointed
- Abstentions: recorded but typically do not count as votes for or against
- Chair's casting vote: apply as specified in the Rules for the relevant organ

**Procedural motions** (points of order, procedural amendments): Ruled on by the Chair;
may be subject to immediate vote by members — follow organ-specific Rules.

## Resolutions — Format & Register

**Standard resolution format**:
```
RESOLUTION [NUMBER]/[YEAR]
[Name of organ], meeting at [venue] on [date]:

RECALLING / REAFFIRMING [prior relevant decisions or commitments];
NOTING / RECOGNISING [relevant facts or context];
CONCERNED / ALARMED [where expressing concern];
DECIDES / RECOMMENDS / URGES / CALLS UPON [operative paragraph — what is being decided];
REQUESTS the Secretariat / [other body] to [specific action] by [deadline];
FURTHER DECIDES [additional operative paragraph if needed].

Adopted at [venue] on [date].
```

**Numbering convention**: Use organ abbreviation + year + sequential number:
- Plenary: `PL-YYYY-NNN`
- EXCO: `EC-YYYY-NNN`
- Standing Committee (by name): `SC[code]-YYYY-NNN`
- Confirm current convention with Secretariat if not established.

**Resolution Register entry**:

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Resolution No. | Per numbering convention |
| Date Adopted | Session date |
| Full Text | Verbatim |
| Responsible Unit | Department/body for implementation |
| Implementation Deadline | Specific date or reporting period |
| Status | PENDING / IN PROGRESS / IMPLEMENTED / LAPSED / REFERRED |
| Next Reporting Date | When implementation is reported back to the organ |

## Minutes Requirements

**Post-meeting checklist**:
- [ ] Minutes accurately reflect all agenda items, discussion summaries, and decisions
- [ ] All resolutions recorded verbatim (or by reference to the adopted text)
- [ ] Vote counts recorded where a vote was taken (for/against/abstentions)
- [ ] Quorum status at opening and any quorum breaks recorded with exact times
- [ ] Attendance list (delegates/members present + observers/guests) finalised and attached
- [ ] Points of order and rulings of the Chair recorded
- [ ] Responsible units assigned for all implementation action items
- [ ] Draft minutes circulated to participants within required timeframe for review

**Adoption of minutes**: Draft minutes are presented for adoption at the next session of
the same organ. Once adopted (with any corrections noted), minutes become an official record.
Adopted minutes are locked — corrections to adopted minutes require a formal motion and
adoption process at a subsequent session.

## Parliamentary Business Coordination

**Documents required for each session** (prepared by Secretariat):
- Provisional agenda (with notice)
- Order paper (final agenda for session)
- Credentials list (Plenary only)
- Background papers for each agenda item
- Committee reports (if applicable)
- Draft resolutions (tabled by proposers in the required format)
- Budget documents (for budget sessions)
- Previous minutes (for adoption)
- List of decisions/resolutions pending implementation (progress report)

**Secretariat coordination responsibilities**:
- Issue meeting notices to all members within required period
- Ensure all documents are translated into working languages before distribution
- Prepare the venue and procedural support (tabling documents, name placards, voting)
- Record minutes and maintain the official record
- Follow up on implementation of decisions after the session

## Policy Implementation Oversight

After adoption of a resolution or decision:

1. **Assignment**: Secretariat assigns each action item to the responsible unit/officer
2. **Tracking**: Maintained in the Resolution Register with deadlines
3. **Reporting**: Progress reported to the next relevant session of the organ
4. **Escalation**: Items not implemented by deadline are escalated to SG for explanation
   to the organ at its next session
5. **Closure**: Implementation confirmed by the responsible unit; SG signs off;
   Resolution Register updated to IMPLEMENTED

**Constitutional amendments**: Require specific procedures and majorities defined in the
Constitution. Consult the Constitution and Legal before any amendment process.
