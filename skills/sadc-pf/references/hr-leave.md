## Leave Types & Eligibility

| Leave Type | Key Conditions | Supporting Document Required |
|------------|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Annual Leave** | Accrued per service period; balance must be sufficient | Leave application form |
| **Sick Leave** | Medical certificate required beyond policy threshold (consult Admin Rules for day limit) | Medical certificate from registered practitioner |
| **Study/Examination Leave** | Must be for approved programme relevant to SADC PF work; prior HR approval | Enrolment letter; timetable/examination schedule |
| **Compassionate Leave** | Death or serious illness of immediate family member | Death certificate / hospital documentation |
| **Lieu Leave (TOIL)** | Granted for overtime worked; subject to 30-day rule (see TOIL section) | Overtime approval record |
| **Maternity Leave** | Per Admin Rules entitlement; notification required in advance | Medical confirmation of pregnancy |
| **Paternity Leave** | Per Admin Rules entitlement | Birth certificate or equivalent |
| **Special Leave** | Exceptional circumstances; requires SG/HR Director authorisation | Written motivation; SG/HR Director approval |

> **Important**: Specific entitlement days (e.g., 21 days annual, X days sick) are defined
> in the current edition of the SADC PF Administrative Rules. Always verify current entitlements
> with HR before advising a staff member on their balance.

## Leave Application Process

**Step-by-step**:
1. Staff member completes Leave Application Form
2. Leave type selected; dates specified (from–to, number of working days)
3. Current leave balance confirmed by HR (before approval)
4. Supporting documents attached (type-specific — see table above)
5. Form submitted to Line Manager for recommendation
6. HR reviews balance and policy eligibility
7. Director/SG approves (per grade — see Approval Routing)
8. Approved form filed in personnel file

**Validation checklist**:
- [ ] Leave type valid and staff member eligible
- [ ] Leave balance sufficient (confirmed by HR)
- [ ] Application submitted within required notice period
- [ ] Supporting documents attached
- [ ] Approval routing complete per grade

**Common rejection reasons**:
- Insufficient leave balance
- No supporting documents for sick/compassionate leave
- Notice period not observed
- Same person signing as applicant and approver (segregation of duties violation)

## Leave Accrual & Balance Tracking

- Annual leave accrues progressively over the service year
- Leave taken in excess of accrued balance constitutes a debit balance — recovery may be
  required on separation from service
- Leave balances must be updated in the personnel file after each approved leave period
- At year-end: unused leave may be carried over subject to Admin Rules limits, or encashed
  where policy permits (consult Admin Rules for current rules)

**Leave register format** (to be maintained per staff member):

| Period | Leave Type | Days Taken | Balance Before | Balance After | Approved By |
|--------|-----------|------------|----------------|---------------|-------------|
| | | | | | |

## Overtime Administration

**Fundamental rule**: Overtime MUST be **pre-approved BEFORE it is worked**.

- Retrospective approval is NOT permitted without documented emergency justification
  authorised by the HR Director
- Overtime is only recognised for hours worked beyond normal working hours as defined in
  the Admin Rules
- All overtime must be recorded on a timesheet and countersigned by the Line Manager

**Overtime approval form must capture**:
- Date(s) and hours of overtime to be worked
- Reason/justification (specific task or event)
- Compensation method agreed: **cash payment** or **Time Off In Lieu (TOIL)**
- Line Manager pre-approval signature (before the overtime is worked)

---

## Time-Off-In-Lieu (TOIL)

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Deadline to take TOIL | Within **30 calendar days** of the overtime worked |
| If not taken within 30 days | TOIL **lapses** unless specifically authorised by SG or HR Director to extend |
| Recording requirement | TOIL schedule (target date for leave) must be agreed at the time overtime is approved |
| TOIL tracking | HR maintains a TOIL register with expiry dates visible |

**TOIL tracker format**:
```
Staff Member: [Name]
Overtime Date(s): [DD/MM/YYYY]
Hours Earned: [X hours = X TOIL days]
TOIL Expiry Date: [Overtime date + 30 days]
TOIL Scheduled Date: [Agreed date to take time off]
Status: PENDING / TAKEN / LAPSED / EXTENDED (with authorisation reference)
Authorisation for Extension (if applicable): [Name, Date, Reference]
```

## Personnel File Management

Each staff member's personnel file must contain (at minimum):
- Signed employment contract and any amendments
- Certified copies of qualifications
- All approved leave records (chronological)
- Overtime approval and TOIL records
- Salary advance applications and repayment schedules
- Performance assessments (where applicable)
- Disciplinary records (if any) — marked [CONFIDENTIAL — RESTRICTED ACCESS]
- Medical certificates (if any) — marked [CONFIDENTIAL — RESTRICTED ACCESS]
- Any correspondence affecting terms and conditions of employment

**File access**: Personnel files are restricted to HR Director, the staff member concerned,
and SG where required. No other department (Finance, Procurement, etc.) has access to
disciplinary or medical content without documented authorisation.

## Approval Routing by Grade

General routing (verify grade-specific routing in Admin Rules):

| Request | Routing |
|---------|---------|
| Annual/sick/compassionate leave (professional staff) | Staff → Line Manager (recommend) → HR (verify balance) → Director (approve) |
| Annual/sick leave (support staff) | Staff → Line Manager (approve) → HR (record) |
| Study leave | Staff → Line Manager → HR Director → SG |
| Special leave / maternity / paternity | HR Director → SG |
| Overtime pre-approval | Staff → Line Manager (must approve BEFORE overtime worked) |
| TOIL extension beyond 30 days | Line Manager → HR Director → SG |
| Disciplinary matters | HR Director (with appropriate confidentiality — route to SG as required) |

**Segregation of duties**: The same person cannot both apply for and approve their own leave
or overtime. Flag any instance where the applicant and approver are the same.

## Namibian Labour Act Awareness

The SADC PF operates under Namibian jurisdiction and must be aware of relevant provisions
of the **Namibian Labour Act (Act 11 of 2007 as amended)**:

| Area | Key principle |
|------|--------------|
| Working hours | Standard hours defined; overtime must be by agreement or emergency |
| Leave entitlements | Act sets minimum entitlements — SADC PF Admin Rules may be more generous |
| Sick leave | Statutory minimum sick leave; certificate requirements |
| Maternity leave | Statutory minimum; SADC PF policy may exceed |
| Termination | Notice periods and severance per Act |
| Discrimination | No unfair discrimination in employment decisions |

> **Note**: Where SADC PF Admin Rules are more generous than the Labour Act, the Admin Rules
> apply. Where they are less generous, the Labour Act minimum applies. Consult HR Director
> or Legal for any conflict.

## Confidential Records Handling

All HR records involving personal, disciplinary, medical, or sensitive content must be:

1. Labelled: **[CONFIDENTIAL — RESTRICTED ACCESS]**
2. Stored securely (physical: locked filing cabinet; digital: access-controlled folder)
3. Accessible only to: the staff member, HR Director, SG (where required), Legal (where applicable)
4. Not disclosed to Finance, Procurement, or other departments without documented authorisation

Breach of HR confidentiality may constitute a disciplinary offence under the Admin Rules.

## Record Certification & Validation

- All leave records must be signed by both the staff member and the approving officer
- Approved records are filed in the personnel file within 5 working days of approval
- Any correction to a leave record after approval requires: a note explaining the correction,
  the authorisation reference, and the name/signature of the person making the correction
- Photocopies of supporting documents must be certified as true copies by an authorised officer
  (HR Director or equivalent) where originals are held elsewhere
