## Budget Preparation & Approval

**Budget cycle**:

| Stage | Action | Responsible |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| Budget call | Finance issues guidance to all departments/units | Finance Director |
| Departmental submissions | Each unit submits programme/cost budget with justifications | Heads of Unit |
| Consolidation | Finance consolidates into draft organisational budget | Finance Director |
| SG review | SG reviews and adjusts priorities | SG |
| EXCO review | EXCO reviews and approves for submission to Plenary | EXCO |
| Plenary adoption | Plenary adopts the annual budget | Plenary Assembly |

**Budget document structure**:
- Administrative/Secretariat costs (salaries, operational costs, overheads)
- Programme costs (by programme/committee/activity)
- Capital expenditure (equipment, furniture, IT, vehicles)
- Donor-funded components (separate tracking by donor project)
- Contingency reserve (percentage of total — per Admin Rules)

**Budget amendments**: Significant reallocation between budget lines or addition of
unbudgeted expenditure requires Finance Director approval (for minor reallocations within
a programme) or EXCO/Plenary approval (for material changes — verify thresholds).

## Financial Year Compliance

- The SADC PF financial year runs from **[confirm current financial year dates with Finance]**
  (typically January–December or as set in the Constitution/Admin Rules)
- All expenditures must be committed and approved within the financial year they are budgeted
- Year-end procedures: Finance closes the books, processes accruals, and prepares year-end
  accounts within the period prescribed in the Admin Rules
- Carry-over of unspent donor funds: follow donor-specific rules for carry-over/return
- Staff leave balances and outstanding imprests must be reconciled at year-end

## Fund Authorisation Control

**Approval authority levels** (verify current delegation schedule with Finance/SG office):

| Transaction Type | Authorising Authority |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Day-to-day operational expenditure (within budget) | Finance Director / SG (per delegation) |
| Procurement within approved budget | Per procurement thresholds (see procurement.md) |
| Budget reallocation (minor — within programme) | Finance Director |
| Budget reallocation (material — between programmes) | SG + EXCO approval |
| New expenditure not in approved budget | EXCO approval (or Plenary if material) |
| Staff appointments and remuneration changes | SG + EXCO (per delegation) |
| Capital expenditure | SG + Finance Director (above threshold: EXCO) |

**Commitment vs. payment**: A budget commitment (e.g., signed contract, approved purchase
order) must be recorded against the budget line at the time of commitment — not only when
payment is made. Finance tracks both commitments and actual expenditures.

## Deductions & Allowance Governance

All deductions and allowances must be:
- Authorised by the appropriate organ or officer (per Admin Rules)
- Documented with the specific basis (signed form, resolution, contract)
- Processed through payroll (not as ad hoc cash payments where avoidable)
- Recorded in the audit trail with authorisation reference

**Allowance changes**: Any change to allowance structures (travel, housing, education, etc.)
requires a formal decision of the appropriate governing organ — Finance cannot unilaterally
alter allowances. Changes take effect from the date specified in the organ's decision.

See also:
- `salary-advance.md` for payroll deduction governance
- `travel.md` for DSA and travel allowances
- `hr-leave.md` for leave encashment and TOIL

## Asset Management

**Fixed asset register**: Finance maintains a register of all SADC PF assets with:
- Asset description and unique identifier/tag number
- Date of acquisition
- Purchase cost
- Location / assigned user
- Depreciation schedule (per Admin Rules/accounting policy)
- Disposal date and method (if disposed)

**Asset controls**:
- All assets must be tagged and inventoried
- Annual physical verification of assets against the register (Finance + Admin)
- Any missing asset must be reported immediately and investigated
- Disposal of assets requires Finance Director + SG approval (and EXCO for significant assets)
- No asset may be removed from SADC PF premises for personal use without documented authorisation

**Donor-funded assets**: Assets purchased with donor funds are subject to the donor's
asset management requirements (may include return to donor at project end). Verify with
Finance before disposing of any donor-funded asset.

## External Audit Awareness

The SADC PF is subject to external audit to ensure financial accountability to its
governing organs and donors.

**Audit process**:
- External auditors are appointed by the Plenary (or EXCO per delegation)
- Audit is conducted annually at the end of the financial year
- Auditors review: financial statements, internal controls, compliance with Admin Rules,
  donor reporting, procurement records, payroll

**What auditors look for**:
- Proper authorisation of all expenditures
- Correct classification of expenditures against budget lines
- Original supporting documents for all transactions
- Compliance with procurement thresholds and rules
- Completeness and accuracy of asset register
- Proper payroll controls and segregation of duties
- Timely retirement of travel advances
- Compliance with donor conditions and reporting

**Staff obligations during audit**:
- Provide all requested documents promptly
- Do not conceal or alter any records
- If a document is missing, report it honestly and document the search
- Audit findings (management letter points) must be responded to within the required
  timeframe with an action plan

**Audit findings resolution**:
1. Finance Director reviews audit management letter
2. Response prepared with action plan for each finding
3. SG approves and submits response to auditors/EXCO
4. EXCO reviews audit findings and management response
5. Action plan implementation tracked

## Accountability Reporting

**Internal reporting**:
- Monthly financial reports: budget vs. actual by programme/unit (Finance → SG)
- Quarterly programme implementation reports (Programme → SG → EXCO)
- Annual financial statements (Finance → external auditors → EXCO → Plenary)

**Donor reporting**:
- Each donor project requires financial and narrative reports per the grant agreement schedule
- Reports must be submitted on time — late reporting is a compliance breach that can
  affect future funding
- Finance coordinates with Programme Officers to ensure narrative and financial reports
  are consistent
- Donor reports must be approved by Finance Director and SG before submission

**EXCO and Plenary financial reports**:
- Finance Director presents financial report at each EXCO meeting
- Annual audited accounts presented to Plenary for adoption
- Budget performance report presented to Plenary alongside the next year's budget proposal

## Financial Rules & Regulations Application

Key financial controls (from SADC PF Accounting Manual/Admin Rules):

| Control | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Segregation of duties | No person may authorise, prepare, and record the same transaction |
| Dual signatures | Bank payments above a specified threshold require two authorised signatories |
| Payment vouchers | Every payment requires a completed, approved payment voucher with supporting documents |
| Invoice verification | Invoices must be verified against purchase order/contract before payment |
| Receipt acknowledgement | Receipt of goods/services must be confirmed before payment |
| Petty cash | Maintained within an approved imprest limit; reconciled regularly; top-up approved by Finance Director |
| Bank reconciliation | Monthly bank reconciliation prepared and reviewed by Finance Director |
| No cash advances without authorisation | All advances (travel, salary, programme) require documented prior approval |

**Non-compliance consequences**: As stated in the Admin Rules — non-compliance with
financial rules and regulations may trigger disciplinary action. Finance Director is
responsible for ensuring staff are aware of and comply with financial policies.

## Internal Controls Summary

The SADC PF financial management framework relies on these interlocking controls:

1. **Budget control**: Expenditure only within approved budget lines
2. **Authorisation**: Every payment/commitment authorised by the appropriate authority
3. **Segregation of duties**: Preparer ≠ Approver ≠ Recorder (where possible)
4. **Documentation**: Every transaction supported by original documents
5. **Reconciliation**: Regular reconciliation of accounts, advances, and asset registers
6. **Audit trail**: Immutable record of who did what, when, and on what authority
7. **External oversight**: Annual external audit with EXCO/Plenary review of findings

**When a control is bypassed**: Any instance where a required control is bypassed
(e.g., payment without authorisation, advance without approval) must be:
- Reported to Finance Director immediately
- Investigated
- Reported to SG
- Documented with a remediation plan
- Included in the next internal/external audit report
